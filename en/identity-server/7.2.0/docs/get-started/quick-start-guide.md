## Who uses {{ product_name }}?

- **Application developers** who build comprehensive IAM solutions for organizations.

- **Administrators** or **team leaders** looking for a secure and user-friendly way to onboard and manage users.

- **Data Protection Officers (DPOs)** who need to implement and monitor systems that comply with privacy protection regulations across the organization.

- **Insight managers** who require accurate data on logins, registrations, and user activities to derive meaningful business insights.

## Why {{ product_name }}?

![IAM functionality]({{base_path}}/assets/img/get-started/iam-functionality.png)

- **Unify sign-ins** – {{ product_name }} facilitates single sign-on (SSO), eliminating the need for users to manage multiple credentials across applications.

- **Give the right access to the right people** – Control access to applications based on user roles, groups, and permission levels.

- **Enforce strong authentication** – {{ product_name }} provides multi-factor authentication options such as Email OTP, SMS OTP, and FIDO Passkeys. It also supports adaptive authentication, allowing authentication requirements to be defined based on the user’s device, location, and contextual factors.

- **Empower users to manage their own accounts** – Enable self-registration and allow users to view and manage their own profiles.

- **Manage users and their accounts** – Take full control of the identity lifecycle across your organization. Provision users to and from trusted identity providers and integrate heterogeneous user stores such as LDAP, Active Directory, and JDBC.

- **Secure ever-growing APIs** – {{ product_name }} acts as an authorization server that supports multiple OAuth-related standards and profiles. It supports open standards such as OAuth 2.0, OpenID Connect, and SAML 2.0, while enabling high availability, failover, and optimal performance.

- **Safeguard user data and give users control** – {{ product_name }} enables the recording, review, and revocation of user consent by adhering to privacy-by-design principles and regulatory standards such as GDPR, CCPA, and LGDP.
